About
You are looking at a multiplayer version of John Conway's Game of Life. In this version, players can edit the grid from their browsers simultaneously, and watch each other's changes unfold and interact. The game never resets, so you'll likely see some remnants of past civilizations.

This version also has "competing species". Each cell takes on the most populous neighboring color. If multiple colors are tied, one is chosen at random. This gives rise to "color oscillators" from otherwise still lifes. For example, try drawing a beehive composed of two colors.
